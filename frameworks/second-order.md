# Second-Order Thinking

**Phase:** Decide · **Source:** https://untools.co/second-order-thinking

## Entry Predicate
`always_run`

## Inputs
- Top 2-3 candidate options from Decision Matrix (or Productive Thinking forged solutions if Decision Matrix not yet run)

## Method
For each candidate option, ask "and then what?" three times:
1. **First-order effects**: what happens immediately?
2. **Second-order effects**: as a result of (1), what then happens?
3. **Third-order effects**: as a result of (2), what then happens?

Mark each effect: **likely** / **possible** / **speculative**.

## Output Schema (table)
| Option | 1st-order | 2nd-order | 3rd-order |
|---|---|---|---|
| Option A | <effect> [likely] | <effect> [likely] | <effect> [possible] |
| Option B | ... | ... | ... |

## Decision Hook
Decisions where 2nd or 3rd-order effects flip from positive to negative ("medicine becomes poison") are flagged in Dissent. Decisions with consistent positive effects across orders increase confidence rubric +2.

## What This Means For The Decision
Most decisions are made on first-order effects only. Second-order surfaces the unintended consequences that sink seemingly-good calls. Third-order is where compound effects (good or bad) live.
